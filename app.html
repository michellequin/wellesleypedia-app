  • What exactly constitutes colonialism??

    I don't understand

    • Answers

  • This is what google provided me on a quick search: the policy or practice of acquiring full or partial political control over another country, occupying it with settlers, and exploiting it economically.

  • According to Stanford's Encyclopedia of Philosophy: Colonialism is a practice of domination, which involves the subjugation of one people to another. One of the difficulties in defining colonialism is that it is hard to distinguish it from imperialism. Frequently the two concepts are treated as synonyms.

  • How did APIA gain a reputation for being a model minority?

    • Answers

  • To quote Giselle Chow: "From a distance, Asian American Pacific Islander (AAPI) students appear to be our great success story in education. As a population, they are overrepresented among winners of National Merit Scholarships, U.S. Presidential Scholarships, and are undergraduates at the countrys most prestigious universities."

  • The model minority came out of the racial zero sum game that a lot of white people were trying to perpetuate. Basically, white people used "asians" as token POC to invalidate the anger of black/brown people.

  • It's literally a myth made up because Asians (East asians mostly) did well academically and socioeconomically.

  • Who counts as Asian American?

    Just to clarify

    • Answers

  • Ask yourself the following questions: 1) Are your parents APIA? 2) Are you APIA? If any of these are true, and you choose to identify yourself as APIA, then you're Asian American!

  • I think also transracial adoptees count! As long as you identify as APIA, and have some background in APIA communities, you are definitely Asian American.

  • If you think you're Asian American, then you are. No questions asked.

  • I'm mixed race, and I definitely grappled with this question before! But I can definitely say that as long as you identify as Asian American, you are!! No one should invalidate how Asian you are!

  • What counts as AAVE?

    Sometimes I can't tell what is AAVE or just slang.

    • Answers

  • I'm not sure if there's a consolidated list, but I think we need to listen to black people and their concerns when they call us out on our appropriation of their language.

  • Some words that come to mind are "lit", "gucci","beating your face", "keepin it one hunna", etc. I don't think there's an official list, but there's work being done to document this kind of vernacular on the internet.

  • Dropping of the r's is common for AAVE speech. I think before you say something that might be AAVE, think about where you heard it, whether that be a rap song, or from a black person. If that's the case, maybe reevaluate why you say it.

  • Does anyone have good sources for AAVE dictionaries?

    Just curious.

    • Answers

  • I don't think there's a real definitive list out there, but I'll contribute a few words that I consider AAVE, as a black person: lit, talkin' shit, roll up, and wildin out (wyling out).

  • Dropping your r's in general is probably a sign of AAVE usage.

  • "Beat my face" is one that I see used a lot! Also, "YAAAAS", "Slay", "Savage", and "extra".

  • It really bothers me when people use on fleek, or on point. Also, more minor words that are still AAVE are when people use cat or dog to refer to their friends.

  • This is obvious, but the n word is off limits.
